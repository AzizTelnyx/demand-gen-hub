## 2. Narrative Discipline

All content reinforces the positioning constitution.

Every asset maps to one of three pillars.

**Layer 1: Global Communications (Trust).** AI agents interacting with humans require identity, reputation, and fraud controls. Core themes: call identity verification, call reputation infrastructure, branded calling, AI voice detection, SIM identity layer.

**Layer 2: Voice AI Platform (Infrastructure).** Voice AI systems require telecom infrastructure. Core themes: carrier routing, direct IP connectivity, private connectivity (VXCs), multi-cloud resilience, multi-POP architecture, wireless mobile core.

**Layer 3: Edge Compute (Physics).** Voice AI performance is constrained by network physics. Core themes: latency accumulation, network hops, geographic distance, packet jitter, speed of light limits.

**Narrative Rule:** If content cannot clearly map to Global Communications, Voice AI Platform, or Edge Compute, it does not ship.

## 3. Content Engine

Content production begins with technical insight.

The system:

```
Engineering insight -> PMM technical blog -> AEO article expansion ->
Social distribution -> Developer tutorial / docs -> Sales enablement
```

One insight should produce 5 to 7 assets. This maintains high output while preserving narrative alignment.

## 6. Team Structure

The system only works if ownership is clear.

### PMM (Primary Content Producers)

PMMs drive category narrative and blog velocity. They operate as technical analysts embedded in the company.

Weekly PMM output: 2 blog posts, 2 LinkedIn posts, 1 X thread.

Monthly PMM output: 1 flagship deep dive.

Examples of flagship deep dives: Voice AI Latency Benchmark. Voice AI Infrastructure Architecture. Voice AI Fraud and Trust Report. Voice AI Production Architecture.

With 5 PMMs, that is 10 blogs weekly.

### Engineering

Engineering supplies insight. Latency measurements, routing architecture, scaling lessons.

Expectation: 1 technical insight per team per month. PMM converts insight into blogs. PMM makes sure this happens.

### DevRel

DevRel converts blog material into developer education. Weekly output: 1 to 2 tutorials. Build guides, framework integrations, SDK examples.

### AEO / Search / LLM Authority

AEO runs like a newsroom. Weekly output: 6 to 8 articles targeting category queries. Monthly output: 1 authority pillar asset. Examples: Voice AI Infrastructure Guide, Voice AI Architecture Handbook, Voice AI Latency Benchmark.

Goal: dominate search engines and LLM answer engines.

### Distribution / Social

Distribution ensures insights reach developers and buyers quickly. Weekly output: 10 to 14 X posts, 6 LinkedIn posts, 3 developer community posts, 2 video clips or demos.

### Paid (2 operators covering global time zones)

Weekly: 5 creative or targeting experiments.

Monthly: 4 new campaign angles tied to current narrative phase.

Regional targeting priorities:
- NORAM: AI startups and voice agent builders
- EU: compliance and regulated industries
- MENA: enterprise and government AI adoption
- APAC: developer ecosystems and infrastructure scale
- LATAM: cost-efficient AI infrastructure and telecom integration

## 8. Blog Structure Template

Every technical blog follows this structure.

1. **Hook.** State the technical insight. Example: "Voice AI latency is primarily a network problem."
2. **Current Architecture.** Explain the Frankenstack.
3. **The Problem.** Where the architecture fails.
4. **Telnyx Architecture.** Explain the integrated infrastructure model.
5. **Proof.** Benchmarks, diagrams, measurements.
6. **Implementation.** Show how developers build with it.

---

## 9. Content Quality Discipline

High output must not degrade quality.

**No Feature Announcement Spam.** Banned post type: "Introducing Feature X." Features must appear inside architecture insights. Bad: "Introducing Telnyx STT Router." Good: "Why Voice AI Needs Speech Routing Infrastructure."

**No Stock Imagery.** Stock photography is banned. Generic tech photos, AI brain graphics, abstract cloud imagery. All banned.

**Diagram Requirement.** Every major blog includes architecture diagrams, network flow diagrams, or latency charts.

**Diagram Library.** Core reusable visuals: Voice AI pipeline. Frankenstack architecture. Integrated infrastructure architecture. Latency accumulation diagram. Trust architecture. Telecom routing path. These diagrams appear across blogs, social posts, docs, and sales decks.

---

## 10. Social Strategy

Social distributes insights. It does not invent narrative.

**X Strategy.** Audience: developers. Cadence: daily. Post types: technical threads, architecture diagrams, latency charts, debate posts. Example: "Voice AI latency is not an LLM problem. It is a network problem."

**LinkedIn Strategy.** Audience: founders, product leaders, enterprise buyers. Cadence: 5 to 6 posts weekly. Style: category framing. Example: "Everyone is optimizing AI models for voice agents. But the biggest source of delay in voice AI systems is the network path between vendors."

**Developer Communities.** Distribution team posts to Hacker News, Reddit developer forums, and Discord developer communities. Focus: technical discussion, not marketing.
