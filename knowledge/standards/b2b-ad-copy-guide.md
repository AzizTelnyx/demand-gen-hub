# B2B Ad Copy Guide - 2026
## Copy Specifications & Writing Best Practices

## Google Ads

### Responsive Search Ads (RSA)

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headlines:** 30 characters each, up to 15 headlines maximum
- **Descriptions:** 90 characters each, up to 4 descriptions maximum
- **Display URL Paths:** 15 characters each, maximum 2 paths

**Display Rules:**
- Google shows up to 3 headlines and 2 descriptions at once
- Headlines must work independently (descriptions don't always show)

**Character Rules:**
- **Allowed:** periods (.), commas (,), exclamation points (!), question marks (?), apostrophes ('), quotation marks ("), hyphens (-), slashes (/), parentheses (), colons (:), semicolons (;), ampersands (&), plus signs (+), dollar signs ($), percent signs (%), trademark symbols (™, ®)
- **Prohibited:** emojis, excessive punctuation (!!!, ???), decorative symbols, arrows (→), stars (★), unusual Unicode
- No repetitive punctuation or excessive capitalization
- Spaces count as characters

**Double-Width Languages:**
- Korean, Japanese, Chinese: each character counts as 2 toward limit

### Performance Max

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headlines:** 3-15 headlines, 30 characters each
- **Long Headlines:** 1-5 long headlines, 90 characters each
- **Descriptions:** One 60-character description (required) + 1-4 descriptions at 90 characters each
- **Business Name:** 1 required, 25 characters
- **Display URL Path:** 15 characters, maximum 2 paths

### Responsive Display Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Short Headlines:** 30 characters, provide 3-5 variations
- **Long Headline:** 90 characters, 1 variation
- **Descriptions:** 90 characters, provide 3-5 variations
- **Business Name:** 25 characters

### Video Ads (YouTube)

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headlines:** 30 characters
- **Descriptions:** 90 characters

### Google Ads Copywriting Best Practices

**Headlines:**
- **Lead with benefits, not features:** "Save 40% on international calls" vs "Advanced VoIP technology"
- **Include numbers and specifics:** "10,000+ businesses trust us" vs "Many businesses trust us"
- **Front-load important information:** Most critical words first (truncation may occur)
- **Create urgency when appropriate:** "Limited Q1 offer" "New in 2026"
- **Match keyword intent exactly:** If they search "enterprise CPaaS," headline should include "enterprise CPaaS"
- **Use power words:** Proven, guaranteed, certified, trusted, industry-leading, award-winning
- **Provide 8-15 unique headline variations** for optimal testing

**Headline Examples:**
- Product-focused: "Enterprise CPaaS API Platform"
- Value-focused: "99.999% Uptime SLA Guaranteed"
- Social proof: "Trusted by 10,000+ Companies"
- Urgency: "Q1 Special: 25% Off Setup Fees"
- Pain point: "Never Miss Customer Calls Again"

**Descriptions:**
- **First description is critical:** May be only one shown
- **Address pain points directly:** "Struggling with call quality? Our platform delivers crystal-clear voice."
- **Include trust signals:** Certifications, customer count, ratings, awards, compliance
- **Different descriptions for different intents:**
  - Problem-aware: Focus on pain points solved
  - Solution-aware: Feature comparisons, differentiators
  - Ready-to-buy: Pricing clarity, ease of setup, support quality
- **Strong call-to-action:** "Start free trial" "Get instant quote" "Book demo today"
- **Specific value propositions:** "24/7 expert support" "No credit card required" "Setup in 5 minutes"

**Description Examples:**
- Trust-focused: "SOC 2 Type II certified. Trusted by Fortune 500 companies. 24/7 expert support included."
- Feature-focused: "Support 100M+ API calls/day. Global carrier network. Real-time analytics dashboard."
- Benefit-focused: "Reduce telecom costs 40%. Scale instantly. Deploy in any region worldwide."

**Tone & Style:**
- **B2B Professional:** Authoritative but not stuffy
- **Clear and Concise:** Every character counts
- **Action-oriented:** Start with verbs where possible
- **Specific over vague:** "Save 40%" not "Save money"
- **Avoid jargon** unless targeting technical buyers who expect it

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Generic headlines that could apply to any company
- Repetitive messaging across headlines
- Wasting characters on "Welcome" or "Hello"
- Over-capitalizing words
- Ending with unnecessary punctuation
- Vague value propositions

## LinkedIn Ads

### Single Image Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline:** 70 characters (200 max, but keep under 70 to prevent truncation)
- **Introductory Text:** 150 characters recommended for mobile (600 max)
- **Description (LAN only):** 70 characters (only if using LinkedIn Audience Network)

**CTA Options (Preset):**
Apply, Download, View Quote, Learn More, Subscribe, Register, Join, Attend, Request Demo, Sign Up

### Video Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline:** 70 characters (200 max)
- **Introductory Text:** 150 characters recommended (600 max)

### Carousel Image Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Introductory Text:** 130-150 characters for mobile (255 max)
- **Card Headline (with landing page):** 45 characters max
- **Card Headline (with Lead Gen Form):** 30 characters max

### Document Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline:** 70 characters
- **Introductory Text:** 150 characters recommended

### Message Ads (Sponsored InMail)

**Text Specifications:**
- **Subject Line:** 60 characters max
- **Body Message:** 1,500 characters max (keep short for best performance)
- **CTA Text:** 20 characters max
- **Hyperlinked text:** 70 characters max

**Best Practices:**
- Personalize with {firstName} macro
- Keep message conversational and brief
- Use up to 3 links maximum
- Up to 10 emojis allowed

### Conversation Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Body Message:** 8,000 characters max (keep simple and conversational)
- **Multiple CTA buttons available**

### Text Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline:** 25 characters
- **Description:** 75 characters

### LinkedIn Copywriting Best Practices

**Headlines:**
- **Short and direct:** 2-4 words ideal
- **Avoid corporate speak:** "2026 SaaS Benchmarks" vs "Our Comprehensive Guide to SaaS Industry Trends"
- **Lead with value:** What will they learn or gain?
- **Use numbers:** "5 Ways to Reduce Cloud Costs" not "Ways to Reduce Cloud Costs"
- **Create curiosity:** "The API Mistake Costing You 40%"

**Headline Examples:**
- Direct value: "Free CPaaS ROI Calculator"
- Curiosity: "Why Enterprise Teams Switch to Telnyx"
- Social proof: "Join 10,000+ Development Teams"
- Timely: "2026 Communications Platform Buyer's Guide"
- Pain point: "Fix Your Call Quality Issues"

**Introductory Text:**
- **First 150 characters critical:** This shows before "See more" on mobile
- **Value proposition upfront:** Don't bury the lead
- **Conversational tone:** Write like a human, not a corporation
- **Address pain point:** "Still dealing with dropped calls?"
- **Preview the benefit:** "Learn how to reduce telecom costs 40%"
- **Avoid promotional fluff:** Get to the point immediately

**Introductory Text Examples:**
- Pain point lead: "Dropped calls frustrating your customers? Our enterprise CPaaS platform delivers 99.999% uptime. See how teams like yours switched and saved 40% on telecom costs."
- Value lead: "Download our 2026 Communications Platform Guide. Compare features, pricing, and real customer reviews. Make the right choice for your team."
- Social proof lead: "Why did 10,000+ developers choose Telnyx? Superior call quality, global reach, and half the cost. See the difference in our interactive demo."

**Tone & Voice:**
- **Authentic over polished:** LinkedIn users tired of corporate-speak
- **Human-centric:** Real people, real benefits
- **Professional but approachable:** Authoritative without being stiff
- **Benefit-focused over feature-focused:** "Scale globally in minutes" not "Multi-region infrastructure"
- **Specific and quantifiable:** "Save 40%" not "Save money"

**Message Ads Specific:**
- **Subject line = email subject:** Make it compelling
- **Greeting with name:** "Hi {firstName},"
- **Short paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences maximum per paragraph
- **Clear single ask:** One primary CTA
- **Personal signature:** From a real person, not company

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Leading with company name or product name
- Feature lists without context or benefits
- Corporate jargon and buzzwords
- Vague value propositions
- No clear call-to-action
- Writing past 150 characters before stating value

**CTA Best Practices:**
- **Match to funnel stage:**
  - Top of funnel: "Download Guide" "Watch Video"
  - Middle of funnel: "See Pricing" "Compare Plans"
  - Bottom of funnel: "Book Demo" "Start Free Trial"
- **Be specific:** "Get ROI Calculator" vs "Learn More"
- **Create urgency:** "Register for Feb 28 Webinar"
- **Remove friction:** "No credit card required"

## StackAdapt

### Native Display Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline:** 55 characters maximum, 15 characters minimum (including spaces)
- **Description:** 120 characters maximum, 25 characters minimum (including spaces)
- **Sponsored By:** 25 characters maximum
- **Call-to-Action:** 10 characters maximum

**Important:** Native ads should blend with publisher content - no overly promotional language

### StackAdapt Copywriting Best Practices

**Headlines:**
- **Concise and intriguing:** 50 characters or under recommended
- **Match editorial style:** Should feel like article headline, not ad
- **Clear summary:** User should know what they'll get when clicking
- **Avoid clickbait:** Deliver on promise
- **No promotional language:** "Free," "Buy now" feel out of place

**Headline Examples:**
- Editorial style: "5 Ways Enterprise Teams Cut Communication Costs in 2026"
- Value-focused: "The Complete Guide to Choosing a CPaaS Platform"
- Problem-solution: "How to Scale Customer Communication Without Hiring"
- Trend-based: "Why Developers Are Moving Away from Legacy Phone Systems"

**Descriptions:**
- **120 character limit is tight:** Every word must earn its place
- **Expand on headline:** Provide additional context
- **Conversational tone:** Match publisher's editorial voice
- **No hard sells:** Informational, not promotional
- **Benefit-oriented:** What will reader learn or gain?

**Description Examples:**
- "Compare features, pricing, and real customer reviews before you buy. Make an informed decision for your team's communication stack."
- "Learn from 10,000+ dev teams who switched and saved 40% on telecom. Get implementation tips, ROI data, and migration checklists."
- "Interactive calculator shows your potential savings. Enter your current usage and get instant cost comparison across top providers."

**Call-to-Action (10 characters):**
- "Read More"
- "Learn How"
- "Get Guide"
- "See Report"
- "Try Free"
- "Compare"
- "Download"

**Sponsored By (25 characters):**
- Usually company name
- Keep it simple and clear
- "Telnyx"
- "Telnyx CPaaS"

**Tone & Style:**
- **Educational, not promotional:** You're providing value, not selling
- **Match publisher voice:** Blend in with editorial content
- **Informative:** Answer questions, solve problems
- **Trustworthy:** No exaggeration or hype
- **Professional:** B2B audience expects substance

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Overly promotional language ("Buy now!" "Limited time!")
- Clickbait headlines that don't deliver
- Feature dumps without benefits
- Sales-heavy language
- ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- Text overlays on images (keep text in copy fields)

**Content Approach:**
- **Top of funnel:** Educational content, industry trends, how-to guides
- **Ungated preferred:** Interactive landing pages, blog posts, articles
- **Not bottom-funnel:** Don't push demo requests or form fills in native ads
- **Value first:** Help the reader, earn their trust

## Reddit Ads

### Image & Video Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline (Feed):** 150 characters maximum
- **Headline (Conversation Placement):** 100 characters maximum
- **Keep under 100 characters** if running multi-placement campaigns

### Carousel Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- **Headline:** 300 characters maximum
- **Caption:** 50 characters maximum

### Free Form Ads

**Text Specifications:**
- Text-only ads
- Character limits vary by subreddit
- Must follow subreddit rules and tone

### Reddit Copywriting Best Practices

**Headlines:**
- **Conversational, not corporate:** "We built a tool to fix [problem], want your thoughts?" vs "Introducing Our Revolutionary Platform"
- **Lead with pain point or value:** Focus on what user gets, not what you're selling
- **Match community tone:** Some subreddits prefer humor, others serious discussion
- **Be authentic:** Redditors detect corporate-speak instantly
- **Short and direct:** Especially for conversation placements (100 char limit)
- **Question format often works:** "Still paying too much for business phone service?"

**Headline Examples:**
- Conversational: "We created a calculator to compare CPaaS costs. Thought r/SaaS might find it useful."
- Problem-focused: "Frustrated by unreliable API uptime? Here's what we learned after 3 years."
- Value-led: "Free tool: See what you're overpaying for telecom [Interactive Calculator]"
- Discussion starter: "What's your biggest frustration with current communication APIs?"
- Authentic: "We're building a developer-first CPaaS. What features matter most to you?"

**Body Text:**
- **Native tone essential:** Sound like a community member, not advertiser
- **Provide value upfront:** Don't make users work to understand benefit
- **Be transparent:** Users know it's an ad, don't try to hide it
- **Educational over promotional:** Share insights, data, learnings
- **Invite discussion:** "What's your experience with X?"
- **No corporate jargon:** Write like a real person

**Tone & Voice:**
- **Authentic and honest:** Redditors value transparency
- **Helpful, not salesy:** You're solving problems, not pushing products
- **Community-minded:** Contribute to discussion, don't interrupt it
- **Self-aware:** Okay to acknowledge you're a business with something to offer
- **Conversational:** First-person ("we," "our") feels more genuine
- **Humble:** Avoid superlatives and hype

**What Works on Reddit:**
- Educational content and how-to guides
- Free tools (calculators, templates, checklists)
- Behind-the-scenes or "lessons learned" content
- Industry benchmarks and data
- Honest comparisons (including competitors)
- Questions that spark discussion
- Content that adds value to the conversation

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Corporate marketing speak
- Aggressive sales pitches
- Vague promises
- Interrupting discussions with irrelevant promotions
- Hiding that you're a business
- Generic stock content
- Excessive punctuation or ALL CAPS

**Conversation Placement Specific:**
- **100 character headline limit:** Even more concise
- **Match discussion topic:** Your ad should feel like continuation of conversation
- **Add value to thread:** Provide information relevant to original post
- **Educational angle:** "Here's what we learned about [topic]"
- **Be prepared to engage in comments**

**CTA Best Practices:**
- **Soft CTAs work better:** "Check it out if interested" vs "Buy now!"
- **Remove friction:** "No signup required" "Free to use"
- **Specific benefit:** "Get instant cost comparison" not "Learn more"
- **Optional feel:** "Might be helpful if..." not "You must..."

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Corporate buzzwords and jargon
- Overly promotional language
- Excessive capitalization or punctuation!!!
- Generic ad copy that could run anywhere
- Ignoring subreddit culture and tone
- Being too sales-heavy too quickly
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver
- Pretending to be a regular user when you're not

**Subreddit-Specific Considerations:**
- **Read subreddit rules before posting:** Some ban promotional content
- **Lurk before you advertise:** Understand community tone
- **Match the vibe:** Technical subreddits want depth, others want accessibility
- **Respect the community:** Add value, don't just extract attention

## General B2B Ad Copy Principles (All Platforms)

### Universal Best Practices

**Clarity Over Cleverness:**
- B2B buyers want clear information
- Clever wordplay rarely converts
- State benefits directly
- Remove ambiguity

**Specificity Wins:**
- "Reduce costs by 40%" beats "Save money"
- "10,000+ customers" beats "Many customers"
- "Deploy in 5 minutes" beats "Quick setup"
- "99.999% uptime" beats "Reliable"

**Benefits Before Features:**
- "Scale globally without infrastructure costs" before "Multi-region data centers"
- "Never miss a customer call" before "Advanced call routing"
- "Cut telecom costs 40%" before "Competitive pricing"

**Address Pain Points:**
- Lead with problems you solve
- "Still dealing with dropped calls?" vs "We have good call quality"
- Acknowledge current frustrations
- Position solution as relief

**Social Proof & Trust Signals:**
- Customer counts: "10,000+ businesses"
- Certifications: "SOC 2 Type II certified"
- Ratings: "4.8/5 stars on G2"
- Notable customers: "Trusted by Fortune 500"
- Awards: "Named Leader by Gartner"

**Strong Calls-to-Action:**
- Specific action: "Download ROI calculator" not "Learn more"
- Remove friction: "No credit card required"
- Create urgency when appropriate: "Register for Feb 28 webinar"
- Match funnel stage: "Compare features" (early) vs "Start free trial" (late)

**Writing Style:**
- Active voice over passive
- Present tense when possible
- Short sentences
- Power words: proven, guaranteed, certified, trusted, industry-leading
- Avoid: jargon (unless audience expects it), vague claims, unnecessary words

### Platform-Specific Tone Adjustments

**Google Ads:**
- Most direct and transactional
- User has high intent, is actively searching
- Get to value proposition immediately
- Feature-focused often works (they're researching specifics)

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional but human
- Thought leadership angle
- Peer-to-peer tone ("teams like yours")
- Balance authority with approachability

**StackAdapt:**
- Editorial, informative
- Blend with publisher content
- Educational over promotional
- Trustworthy journalist tone

**Reddit:**
- Most casual and conversational
- Peer-to-peer discussion
- Authentic and transparent
- Community member, not advertiser

### Character Limit Strategy

**When You Have More Space (LinkedIn 600, Message Ads 1500):**
- Front-load value in first 150 characters
- Expand with supporting details
- Include multiple benefits
- Build case with specifics

**When Space is Tight (Google 30, LinkedIn 70):**
- Every word must earn its place
- Remove articles (a, an, the) if needed
- Use symbols when appropriate (& vs "and", % vs "percent")
- Front-load most important word

**Testing Mindset:**
- Different audiences respond to different messages
- What works in one industry may not in another
- Test benefit-focused vs feature-focused
- Test pain point vs aspiration
- Test specific numbers vs general claims

### Compliance & Legal

**Required Disclaimers:**
- Factor into character counts
- Place in description when possible (not headline)
- Keep concise

**Accuracy:**
- All claims must be verifiable
- "Up to" or "average" when appropriate
- No exaggeration or misleading statements

**Competitor References:**
- Allowed on Google (competitor keywords)
- Generally avoid on LinkedIn/Reddit
- Must be factual if included
- Never disparage
