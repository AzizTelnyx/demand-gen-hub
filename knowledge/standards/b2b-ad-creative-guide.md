## Google Ads

### Responsive Search Ads - Image Assets

**Image Specifications:**
- **Aspect Ratios:** Square (1:1) and Landscape (1.91:1)
- **Minimum Dimensions:** 300×300px
- **File Size Limit:** 5120KB (5MB)
- **File Types:** JPG, PNG, GIF (non-animated)

**Best Practices:**
- Images enhance search ads but are optional
- High quality, clear visuals
- No excessive text overlay
- Product or service in context

### Performance Max - Image Assets

**Image Requirements:**
- **Square (1:1) - REQUIRED:** 
  - 1 minimum, 20 maximum
  - 1200×1200px recommended
  - Minimum 300×300px
- **Portrait (4:5):** 
  - 0 minimum, 20 maximum
  - 960×1200px recommended
- **Landscape Logo (4:1):** 
  - 0 minimum, 5 maximum
  - 1200×300px recommended (minimum 512×128px)

**File Specifications:**
- **File Types:** JPG, PNG, GIF (non-animated)
- **Max File Size:** 5MB per image
- **Resolution:** High quality, 300 DPI recommended

**Logo Requirements:**
- **Square Logo (1:1):** 1200×1200px recommended
- **Transparent Background:** May be rendered on white background
- **Avoid:** Predominantly white logo on transparent background
- **Quality:** Clear, high-resolution to avoid flagging for blurriness

**Image Enhancement Feature:**
- Google AI can automatically fetch images from landing pages
- Can automatically crop images for optimal display
- Opt-in feature

### Responsive Display Ads

**Image Specifications:**
- **Landscape (1.91:1):** 
  - 1200×628px minimum, 1200×628px recommended
  - 600×314px absolute minimum
- **Square (1:1):** 
  - 1200×1200px recommended
  - 300×300px minimum

**File Specifications:**
- **File Types:** JPG, PNG, static GIF
- **Max File Size:** 5MB
- **Important:** Avoid text overlays - Google may add headlines and branding automatically

**Logo Specifications:**
- **Square (1:1):** 1200×1200px recommended
- **Landscape (4:1):** 1200×300px recommended
- Max 5 logos

**Video (Optional):**
- Hosted on YouTube
- **Aspect Ratios:** 16:9, 1:1, 9:16

### Display Ads (Custom Uploaded)

**Priority Sizes for Maximum Reach:**

**Mobile:**
- 300×250 (Medium Rectangle)
- 320×50 (Mobile Banner)
- 320×100 (Large Mobile Banner)

**Desktop:**
- 728×90 (Leaderboard)
- 300×250 (Medium Rectangle) - works on both
- 160×600 (Wide Skyscraper)
- 300×600 (Half Page)

**Universal Best Performer:**
- **300×250** (Medium Rectangle) - Highest reach across mobile and desktop

**File Specifications:**
- **File Types:** JPG, PNG, GIF
- **Max File Size:** Varies by size, typically 150KB
- **Animation:** 30 seconds max for animated GIF

**HTML5 Ads:**
- **ZIP File:** Max 512 files, 20 files per ad group
- **Formats in ZIP:** HTML, CSS, JS, GIF, PNG, JPG, JPEG, SVG
- **Optimal Length:** 90 seconds
- **No:** ZIP files within ZIP files
- Use Google Ads HTML5 Validator tool before upload

### Video Ads (YouTube)

**Video Specifications:**
- **Formats:** Skippable in-stream, non-skippable (15 sec max), bumper (6 sec max)
- **All videos must be hosted on YouTube**
- **Aspect Ratios:** 16:9 (landscape), 1:1 (square), 9:16 (vertical for Shorts)
- **Recommended:** Create both horizontal and vertical versions for maximum reach

**Technical Specs:**
- **Custom Thumbnails:** 1280×720px minimum
- **Companion Banners (Desktop):** 300×60px
- **File Format:** MP4 recommended (H.264 compression, AAC audio)
- **Captions:** Include for better performance (80% watch without sound)

### Google Ads Creative Best Practices

**Image Design Principles:**
- **High quality, sharp resolution:** Avoid blurry or pixelated images
- **Minimal text overlay:** Let headlines and descriptions carry messaging
- **Product/service in context:** Show how it's used, not just product shot
- **Professional but authentic:** Real usage > stock photography
- **Color contrast:** Ensure images stand out in feed
- **Branding:** Logo visible but not overwhelming

**What Works:**
- Product demonstrations
- People using product/service
- Data visualizations (charts, graphs with clear takeaways)
- Before/after comparisons
- Dashboard or interface screenshots (for SaaS)
- Team or workplace imagery (for B2B services)

**What to Avoid:**
- Text-heavy images (Google may add text)
- Cluttered compositions
- Low-resolution images
- Generic stock photos that could apply to anyone
- Images with baked-in CTAs or offers (put in copy instead)

**Video Best Practices:**
- **Hook in first 3 seconds:** Capture attention immediately
- **Include captions:** 80% watch without sound
- **Show product in action:** Demonstrations > talking heads
- **Keep it concise:** 15-30 seconds optimal for most formats
- **Vertical video increasingly important:** Optimize for mobile/Shorts
- **Clear branding:** Logo visible within first few seconds
- **End with CTA:** Clear next step

## LinkedIn Ads

### Single Image Ads

**Image Specifications:**

**Horizontal/Landscape (1.91:1):**
- **Recommended:** 1200×628px
- **Minimum:** 640×360px
- **Maximum:** 7680×4320px
- **Displays:** Desktop and mobile

**Square (1:1) - RECOMMENDED:**
- **Recommended:** 1200×1200px
- **Minimum:** 360×360px
- **Maximum:** 4320×4320px
- **Displays:** Desktop and mobile
- **Best for:** Both platforms, maximum flexibility

**Vertical (1:1.91 or 4:5):**
- **1:1.91:** 628×1200px, 600×900px (2:3), 720×900px (4:5 - recommended to avoid borders)
- **Minimum:** 360×640px
- **Maximum:** 2430×4320px
- **Displays:** Mobile only (not shown on desktop)
- **Performance:** Best CTR on mobile

**File Specifications:**
- **File Types:** JPG, PNG, non-animated GIF
- **Max File Size:** 5MB
- **Note:** 80% of LinkedIn traffic is mobile - vertical images recommended

### Carousel Image Ads

**Image Specifications:**
- **Number of Cards:** 2 minimum, 10 maximum
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1 (square only)
- **Dimensions:** 1080×1080px for each card
- **File Type:** JPG, PNG
- **Max File Size:** 20MB per image

**Design Consistency:**
- Maintain visual cohesion across cards
- Consistent color scheme and branding
- Tell a sequential story or showcase multiple products
- Final card should include strong CTA

### Video Ads

**Video Specifications:**
- **Aspect Ratios:** 
  - 16:9 (landscape) - 1920×1080px recommended
  - 1:1 (square)
  - 9:16 (vertical) - recommended for mobile optimization
- **File Type:** MP4, MOV
- **Min/Max Duration:** 3 seconds min, 30 minutes max (15-30 seconds optimal)
- **Min/Max File Size:** 75KB min, 200GB max
- **Frame Rate:** 30 FPS

**Important:**
- **91% of LinkedIn users watch videos on mobile** - prioritize mobile formats
- **Include captions** - Most watch without sound
- **Square format recommended** for delivery across both desktop and mobile

### Document Ads

**Document Specifications:**
- **File Types:** PDF, DOC, DOCX, PPT, PPTX
- **Max File Size:** 100MB
- **Optimal Length:** 3-10 pages for best engagement
- **First page is preview:** Make it compelling

### Message Ads (Sponsored InMail)

**Banner Image (Optional):**
- **Dimensions:** 300×250px
- **File Types:** JPG, GIF (non-animated), PNG
- **Includes:** Click-through URL
- **Note:** Banner shows on desktop inbox only, not in message itself
- **Recommendation:** Always include to reserve ad space

### Conversation Ads

**Banner Image (Optional):**
- **Dimensions:** 300×250px
- **Note:** Non-clickable, desktop only
- **Shows:** In inbox, not in conversation window

### Follower Ads & Spotlight Ads (Dynamic)

**Image Specifications:**
- **Company Logo:** 100×100px
- **User profile photo:** Automatically pulled from LinkedIn profiles
- **Desktop only**

### Text Ads

**Image (Optional):**
- **Dimensions:** 100×100px
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1 square
- **Tip:** Company logo works best due to small size
- Must be clear and identifiable at small size

### LinkedIn Creative Best Practices

**Image Design Principles:**
- **Authentic over polished:** Real people > stock photography
- **Professional quality:** High resolution, well-composed
- **People-focused:** Show real humans (employees, customers, leadership)
- **Mobile-first:** Design for vertical/square viewing
- **Clear focal point:** One main subject, uncluttered
- **Brand-consistent:** Colors, fonts, style align with brand
- **Contextual:** Show product/service in real business setting

**What Works on LinkedIn:**
- **Real team members:** Leadership, employees, customers
- **Office/workplace environments:** Authentic business settings
- **Product in use:** Screenshots, dashboards, interfaces
- **Data visualizations:** Charts showing results or benchmarks
- **Event photos:** Webinars, conferences (builds credibility)
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Company culture, team collaboration
- **User-generated content:** Customer success stories

**What to Avoid:**
- Generic stock photos (everyone uses same images)
- Staged, overly polished corporate imagery
- Text-heavy images (save text for copy fields)
- Cluttered compositions with multiple focal points
- Images that don't relate to the value proposition
- Low-quality or blurry images

**Video Best Practices:**
- **Captions essential:** 91% watch on mobile without sound
- **Hook immediately:** First 3 seconds determine if they keep watching
- **Vertical or square:** Optimized for mobile feed
- **Keep it short:** 15-30 seconds ideal
- **Show faces:** People engage with people
- **Authentic delivery:** Scripted is fine, but should feel natural
- **Clear branding:** Logo in first few seconds
- **End with CTA:** Tell them what to do next

**Document Ads Creative:**
- **First page is critical:** Acts as thumbnail and preview
- **Professional design:** Branded template
- **Scannable content:** Headers, bullet points, visuals
- **Value-dense:** Every page should provide insights
- **Not a sales pitch:** Educational, thought leadership content
- **Include branding:** Company logo, colors throughout
- **Optimal length:** 3-10 pages (longer = lower engagement)

**Carousel Best Practices:**
- **Strong opening card:** Hooks attention immediately
- **Visual consistency:** Same style, color scheme across all cards
- **Progressive storytelling:** Each card builds on previous
- **Vary imagery:** Don't repeat similar photos
- **Final card CTA:** Clear call-to-action on last slide
- **Test order:** Sometimes different sequences perform better
- **Each card stands alone:** User might not swipe through all

## StackAdapt

### Native Display Ads

**Image Specifications:**
- **Accepted Sizes:** 1200×628px, 600×600px, 800×600px
- **File Formats:** JPG, PNG, GIF
- **Optimal File Size:** 750KB
- **Resolution:** 300 pixels per inch minimum (high resolution required)

**Critical Rule:**
- **NO TEXT OVERLAY on images**
- Images should be clean, editorial-style
- Text belongs in headline/description fields, not on image

### Display Ads (Standard)

**Standard IAB Sizes:**
- 300×250 (Medium Rectangle)
- 728×90 (Leaderboard)
- 160×600 (Wide Skyscraper)
- 300×600 (Half Page)
- 320×50 (Mobile Banner)

### Video Ads

**Video Specifications:**
- **File Formats:** MP4, MOV
- **Recommended Length:** 15-30 seconds
- **Aspect Ratios:** 16:9, 1:1, 9:16
- **Quality:** High resolution

### StackAdapt Creative Best Practices

**Native Ad Image Principles:**
- **Editorial quality:** Should look like it belongs on publisher's site
- **No promotional elements:** No "Sale!" badges, no price overlays, no CTAs on image
- **High-quality photography:** Professional, sharp, well-composed
- **Contextual relevance:** Image should relate to headline/topic
- **Clean and uncluttered:** Single focal point
- **No text whatsoever:** Absolutely no text overlay

**What Works for Native:**
- **Professional photography:** Not obviously stock
- **Product in natural context:** Being used, not isolated on white background
- **People in business settings:** But authentic, not staged
- **Data visualizations:** Clean charts or graphs
- **Screenshots:** Dashboards, tools, interfaces (for B2B tech)
- **Workspace imagery:** Modern office, collaboration
- **Technology close-ups:** Clean, professional product shots

**What to Avoid:**
- Any text on image (even small)
- Sale badges, price tags, promotional elements
- "Spammy" stock photography (overly posed, fake business scenes)
- Cluttered compositions
- Low-resolution images
- Images that scream "advertisement"
- Obvious logos or branding plastered on image (subtle OK)

**Blending with Publisher Content:**
- Study where your ads will appear
- Match the visual style of editorial content
- If publisher uses illustrations, consider illustrated creative
- If publisher uses photography, use similar photography style
- Color palettes that fit with publisher's design
- Professional quality that matches publisher standards

**Testing Approach:**
- Test different image styles (photography vs illustrations vs data viz)
- Test with/without people in image
- Test close-up vs wide shots
- Test color schemes (vibrant vs muted)
- Test product-focused vs lifestyle-focused

## Reddit Ads

### Image Ads

**Image Specifications:**

**Recommended Aspect Ratios:**
- **4:5 Portrait:** 1080×1350px (best for mobile - 70%+ of Reddit usage)
- **1:1 Square:** 1080×1080px
- **16:9 Landscape:** 1920×1080px

**File Specifications:**
- **File Type:** JPG, PNG
- **Max File Size:** 3MB

### Video Ads

**Video Specifications:**
- **Dimensions:** Same as images (1080×1080, 1080×1350, 1920×1080)
- **File Type:** MP4, MOV
- **Max File Size:** 1GB (recommended under 512MB)
- **Duration:** 5-60 seconds recommended (15 minutes max)
- **Frame Rate:** 30 FPS

**Thumbnail:**
- **Dimensions:** 400×300px
- **File Type:** JPG, PNG
- **Max File Size:** 500KB

### Carousel Ads

**Image Specifications:**
- **Number of Cards:** 2-6 images
- **Dimensions:** 1200×1200px (desktop campaigns support 1200×628)
- **File Size:** 20MB max per image
- **Aspect Ratios:** 1:1, 16:9
- **Thumbnail:** 400×300px

### Reddit Creative Best Practices

**Image Design Principles:**
- **Authentic over polished:** Less professional production often performs better
- **User-generated content style:** Should feel organic, not like traditional ads
- **Simple, clean compositions:** Mobile-friendly, easy to read
- **Avoid corporate aesthetic:** Too polished = instant skip
- **Real over staged:** Actual product usage > staged photography
- **Context matters:** Image should relate to subreddit topic

**What Works on Reddit:**
- **Product screenshots:** Real dashboards, tools, interfaces
- **Simple product photography:** Straightforward, honest representation
- **Memes or humor:** If appropriate for your brand and subreddit
- **Data visualizations:** Charts, graphs with interesting insights
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Development, team, real office
- **Comparison images:** Side-by-side product comparisons
- **User-generated content:** Actual customer photos/screenshots
- **Simple text on colored background:** For educational/informational content

**What to Avoid:**
- Overly produced corporate imagery
- Generic stock photography (Redditors can spot it instantly)
- Heavy branding or logo-focused images
- Images with built-in CTAs or promotional text
- Anything that looks like traditional advertising
- Clickbait imagery that doesn't deliver
- Images unrelated to headline or subreddit topic

**Video Best Practices:**
- **Hook in first 3 seconds:** Especially critical on Reddit
- **Authentic, not overly produced:** Raw often performs better
- **Demonstrate real value:** Show actual product, actual results
- **Keep it short:** 15-30 seconds optimal
- **Captions essential:** Most watch without sound
- **Educational angle:** How-to, tutorials, insights
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Development, team, process
- **Avoid:** Talking heads unless personality is compelling

**Subreddit-Specific Considerations:**
- **Technical subreddits:** Screenshots, code, technical diagrams welcome
- **Business subreddits:** Professional but not corporate
- **Design subreddits:** Higher expectations for visual quality
- **Meme subreddits:** Humor is currency (if brand-appropriate)
- **Research the community:** What types of images get upvoted organically?

**Mobile Optimization (Critical):**
- 70%+ of Reddit traffic is mobile
- **4:5 portrait performs best**
- Large, legible elements (no tiny text)
- Single focal point
- Vertical framing for mobile scrolling

## General B2B Creative Principles (All Platforms)

### Universal Design Best Practices

**Image Quality Standards:**
- **High resolution:** 300 DPI minimum when possible
- **Sharp focus:** No blurry images
- **Proper lighting:** Well-lit, professional
- **Color accuracy:** True-to-life colors, not oversaturated
- **File optimization:** Compress for web without visible quality loss

**Composition Fundamentals:**
- **Single focal point:** One main subject per image
- **Rule of thirds:** Position key elements on grid intersections
- **Negative space:** Don't overcrowd, let image breathe
- **Clear hierarchy:** Eye should know where to look first
- **Contrast:** Subject should stand out from background

**Branding Elements:**
- **Logo placement:** Present but not overwhelming
- **Consistent colors:** Use brand color palette
- **Typography:** If text needed, use brand fonts
- **Style consistency:** Maintain visual identity across all ads
- **Subtle branding:** Especially for native/Reddit (too much = turn-off)

### B2B-Specific Visual Strategies

**Professional but Approachable:**
- Avoid sterile, overly corporate aesthetic
- Real people in real business environments
- Modern, clean design without being cold
- Authentic over staged perfection

**People in B2B Imagery:**
- **Show faces:** Humans connect with humans
- **Diverse representation:** Reflects real business world
- **Genuine expressions:** Natural, not forced smiles
- **In context:** Using product, collaborating, problem-solving
- **Eye contact:** Direct gaze or engaged in work (not staring off dramatically)

**Product/Service Visualization:**
- **SaaS/Software:** Dashboard screenshots, interface, data visualizations
- **APIs/Developer tools:** Code snippets, technical diagrams, architecture
- **Services:** Team at work, results/outcomes, case study visuals
- **Physical products:** In use, not just product on white background
- **Show outcomes:** Before/after, results, success metrics

**Data Visualization:**
- Charts and graphs that support value proposition
- Clear, easy-to-read at small sizes
- Highlight key insight or takeaway
- Use brand colors
- Not too complex for ad format
- Source data when appropriate

### Platform-Specific Design Adaptations

**Google Ads:**
- Clean, professional product-focused
- Can be more polished and corporate
- Text-light (Google adds copy)
- Feature-demonstrating imagery works

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional quality essential
- Real people over stock
- Business context important
- Authentic workplace environments

**StackAdapt:**
- Most editorial style
- Must blend with publisher content
- Absolutely no text on image
- High-quality journalism aesthetic

**Reddit:**
- Least polished acceptable
- Authenticity over production value
- User-generated content style
- Avoid corporate aesthetic entirely

### Mobile-First Design Considerations

**All Platforms Trend Mobile:**
- Design for vertical viewing first
- Key elements large enough to see on small screens
- Text (if any) must be readable on mobile
- Single clear focal point
- Avoid tiny details that disappear on mobile

**Aspect Ratio Strategy:**
- **Square (1:1):** Safe for both mobile and desktop
- **Portrait (4:5, 9:16):** Best for mobile, won't show on some desktop placements
- **Landscape (16:9):** Better for desktop, less optimal mobile
- **Create multiple versions:** Optimize for different placements

### Video Creative Principles

**First 3 Seconds Critical:**
- Hook attention immediately
- Show value proposition or intriguing visual
- Branding visible early
- Question, statement, or striking visual

**Captions/Subtitles Essential:**
- 80-91% watch without sound (varies by platform)
- Make video understandable muted
- Use caption/subtitle files or burned-in text

**Length Guidelines:**
- **6 seconds:** Bumper ads, quick brand moments
- **15-30 seconds:** Optimal for most B2B platforms
- **60 seconds:** Maximum for social, use only if needed
- **Shorter often better:** Attention spans are short

**B2B Video Content Types:**
- **Product demonstrations:** Show actual features/functionality
- **Customer testimonials:** Real customers, real results
- **Explainer videos:** How it works, problem-solution
- **Data/results focused:** Show metrics, outcomes, ROI
- **Thought leadership:** Executive insights, industry perspectives
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Team, culture, development process

### A/B Testing Creative Elements

**What to Test:**
- Image style (photography vs illustrations vs data viz)
- With/without people
- Product-focused vs lifestyle/context-focused
- Different color schemes
- Close-up vs wide shots
- Different emotional tones (serious vs lighthearted)
- Video vs static image
- Different video hooks (first 3 seconds)

**Don't Test:**
- Too many variables at once
- Low-quality vs high-quality (always use high quality)
- Brand-inconsistent variations

### File Management Best Practices

**Organization:**
- Clear naming conventions (Platform_Format_Dimension_Version)
- Keep master files separate from exported versions
- Document what performed well
- Maintain asset library for quick creation

**Export Settings:**
- Save in required format for each platform
- Compress to size limits without quality loss
- RGB color mode for digital (not CMYK)
- Remove metadata if needed for file size

### Accessibility Considerations

**Design for Inclusivity:**
- Color contrast for visibility (don't rely on color alone)
- Alt text for images (where platform allows)
- Captions for video
- Clear, readable fonts if text in image
- Consider colorblind-friendly palettes

### Brand Consistency vs Platform Optimization

**Balance:**
- Maintain core brand identity across all platforms
- Adapt execution style to platform norms
- Same brand colors, logo, fonts
- Different levels of polish/production based on platform
- Consistent messaging, variable creative approach

**Platform Adaptation:**
- Professional polish for Google and LinkedIn
- Editorial blend for StackAdapt
- Authentic and raw for Reddit
- Same brand, different creative expression

### Common Creative Mistakes to Avoid

**All Platforms:**
- Low-resolution, blurry images
- Cluttered compositions
- Text-heavy images when platform adds copy
- Generic stock photography everyone uses
- Images unrelated to value proposition
- Ignoring mobile optimization
- Inconsistent branding
- Overly sales-y visual approach
- Videos without captions
- Not testing different creative approaches
