## Headline Structure

### Pinning Strategy
- **Pin 1-2 headlines maximum** (preferably just 1)
- Pin to Position 1 and/or Position 2
- Let remaining headlines rotate for Google to optimize
- Pinning too many limits performance

### What to Pin
**Position 1 options:**
1. Primary value proposition (what we do)
2. Main pain point (what problem we solve)
3. Differentiation (why us vs competitors)

**Position 2: NEVER PIN.** Leave unpinned — each headline must work as a combo with ANY Pin 1 headline. Pinning both H1 and H2 kills rotation (only 2 headlines show).

### Total Headlines
- **Minimum:** 3 headlines
- **Recommended:** 10-15 headlines for optimal performance
- **Maximum:** 15 headlines

### Headline Variety
Include mix of:
- **Pain points:** "When CCaaS Slows You Down"
- **Value props:** "Build AI-Powered Contact Centers"
- **Features:** "Real-Time AI Agent Assist"
- **Benefits:** "No Per-Seat Limits"
- **Differentiators:** "Code vs. Vendor UIs"
- **Social proof:** "Trusted by 500+ Companies" (if applicable)
- **Urgency/Action:** "Start Building Today"

### Character Limits
- **Headlines:** 30 characters max
- **Descriptions:** 90 characters max

## Description Structure

### Total Descriptions
- **Minimum:** 2 descriptions
- **Recommended:** 4 descriptions for optimal performance
- **Maximum:** 4 descriptions

### Description Variety
Include mix of:
1. **Feature + Benefit:** "Embed AI agents into live call flows. Automate safely without breaching SLAs."
2. **Pain + Solution:** "Stop forcing roadmap into vendor UIs. Build routing, IVR, and AI in code."
3. **Differentiation:** "Real-time call logic vs pre-scripted workflows. No per-seat tax."
4. **Credibility:** "Trusted by BPOs and white-label providers globally."

## PPC Ad Copywriting Principles

### SEO vs. PPC Copy
**SEO copy** = informational, optimized for keywords, long-tail phrases ("Best X Alternative")
**PPC copy** = action-oriented, benefit-driven, conversion-focused

**DON'T use SEO patterns in ads:**
- ❌ "Top [Competitor] Alternative"
- ❌ "Best [Category] Tool"
- ❌ "#1 [Product Type]"
- ❌ Generic superlatives without proof

**DO use PPC-optimized copy:**
- ✅ Specific benefits: "Build Faster, Ship Cheaper"
- ✅ Pain points: "When [Competitor] Slows You Down"
- ✅ Outcomes: "Deploy in Minutes, Not Months"
- ✅ Differentiators: "We Own the Network — Real-Time Performance"

### High-Performance PPC Copy Framework

**1. PAIN-AGITATE-SOLVE**
- Pain: "Twilio Costs Killing Your Margins?"
- Agitate: "Per-seat fees add up fast"
- Solve: "Pay-as-you-go pricing saves 60%"

**2. SPECIFICITY WINS**
- ❌ Generic: "Fast voice AI"
- ✅ Specific: "Co-Located Voice + AI Infrastructure"
- ❌ Generic: "Save money"
- ✅ Specific: "60% cheaper than Twilio"

**3. OUTCOME > FEATURE**
- ❌ Feature: "Private global network"
- ✅ Outcome: "Ship voice AI in 48 hours"
- ❌ Feature: "Co-located GPUs"
- ✅ Outcome: "Never lose a call to latency"

**4. MATCH SEARCH INTENT**
- Competitor searches → Show migration ease: "Migrate from Vapi in Minutes"
- Problem searches → Show solution: "Fix Laggy Voice AI Today"
- Product searches → Show differentiation: "Voice AI + Native Telephony"

**5. USE ACTION VERBS**
- Build, Ship, Deploy, Migrate, Scale, Cut, Eliminate, Replace
- Avoid passive: "Can be built" → "Build in minutes"

**6. NUMBERS & PROOF POINTS**
- "Co-located infrastructure" not "ultra-low latency" (NO specific ms numbers — they're arguable)
- "60% cheaper" not "more affordable"
- "140+ countries" not "global coverage"

**7. ADDRESS OBJECTIONS**
- "No per-seat limits" (pricing fear)
- "Migrate in minutes" (switching friction)
- "Zero vendor lock-in" (commitment fear)
- "24/7 expert support" (reliability concern)

### Ad Copy Creation Workflow

### BEFORE writing any ad copy:

1. **Read the landing page**
   - What's the main value prop?
   - What outcomes are promised?
   - What pain points are addressed?
   - What proof points exist? (numbers, speeds, savings)

2. **Identify search intent**
   - Competitor alternative → migration ease + differentiation
   - Problem-solving → outcome + speed
   - Product research → unique capabilities + proof

3. **Read the campaign brief** (if exists)
   - Target audience personas
   - Pain points and messaging pillars
   - Competitor differentiation
   - Performance claims we can make

4. **Check existing campaign** (if updating)
   - What's currently pinned?
   - What's performing well?
   - What needs testing?

### WHEN writing ad copy:

1. **Lead with outcomes, not features**
   - NOT: "Private network and co-located GPUs"
   - YES: "Ship production voice AI in 48 hours"

2. **Use performance language, NOT specific latency numbers**
   - NOT: "Sub-200ms voice latency" or "Sub-300ms latency" (arguable metrics)
   - YES: "Co-Located Voice + AI", "Real-Time Performance Built In", "Production-Grade Reliability"

3. **Address the real pain point**
   - For Vapi alternative: complexity, cost predictability
   - For ElevenLabs alternative: lack of telephony, integration complexity
   - For Twilio alternative: costs, per-seat fees, complexity

4. **Include proof/credibility when available**
   - "Trusted by 500+ companies"
   - "140+ countries supported"
   - "24/7 expert support"

5. **Keep it under character limits**
   - Headlines: 30 chars max
   - Descriptions: 90 chars max
   - Shorter often performs better (20-25 chars for headlines)

6. **Make it action-oriented**
   - "Build voice AI in minutes" not "Voice AI platform"
   - "Cut costs 60% vs Twilio" not "Affordable pricing"

### AFTER writing ad copy:

1. **Cite sources**
   - Which pillar/framework does this come from?
   - Which landing page section?
   - Which brief variant?

2. **Show Aziz before creating**
   - Present the full ad structure
   - Show what's pinned and why
   - Wait for approval

## Pinning Best Practices

### When to Pin
- **Always:** Pin 1 primary headline to Position 1
- **Sometimes:** Pin 1 secondary headline to Position 2 if needed for clarity
- **Never:** Pin more than 2 headlines (kills performance)

### What NOT to Pin
- Don't pin CTAs ("Get Started", "Try Free") - let Google optimize
- Don't pin every headline - defeats the purpose of RSAs
- Don't pin headlines with same meaning ("Build Contact Centers" + "Create Contact Centers")

### Pinning Positions
- **Position 1:** Shows first (most important)
- **Position 2:** Shows second
- **Position 3:** Shows third (rarely use)

### Pin Examples

**Developer Product (Voice AI):**
- Pin 1: "Build Production Voice AI Agents"
- Rotate: Technical benefits, performance language, developer features

**Enterprise Product (Contact Center):**
- Pin 1: "Build AI-Powered Contact Centers"
- Pin 2: "When CCaaS Slows You Down" (optional)
- Rotate: Economics, features, use cases

**Competitor Alternative:**
- Pin 1: "Better Than [Competitor]"
- Rotate: Specific advantages, pricing, features

## Match Types & Keywords

### For Different Funnel Stages

**TOFU (Top of Funnel):**
- Broad awareness keywords
- Phrase match recommended
- Exact match for high-volume terms

**MOFU (Middle of Funnel):**
- Solution-aware keywords
- Mix of phrase and exact match
- More specific than TOFU

**BOFU (Bottom of Funnel):**
- High-intent, specific keywords
- Exact match primarily
- Brand + competitor terms

### Negative Keywords (MANDATORY)
Always include negatives for:
- Jobs/careers (unless hiring campaign)
- Free/cheap (unless free tier campaign)
- Tutorials/courses (unless educational content)
- Wrong intent (industry-specific)

## Landing Page Alignment

### URL Parameters (UTM)
Standard structure:
```
utm_source=google
utm_medium=paid_search
utm_campaign=[campaign_name]
utm_term={keyword}
```

See: ~/clawd/knowledge/standards/utm-tagging-2025.md

### Message Match
- Headline on ad should echo headline on page
- If ad says "Build AI Contact Centers", page should have similar language
- CTA on ad should match CTA on page

### Scent Trail
- Ad → Landing Page → Next Action should be obvious
- Don't send "AI agent" traffic to generic homepage
- Send to specific product page that matches intent

## Testing Strategy

### What to Test
1. **Pinned headline variations** (one at a time)
2. **Pain point vs. benefit** headlines
3. **Feature vs. outcome** descriptions
4. **Short vs. long** headlines (20 chars vs. 28 chars)

### What NOT to Test
- Don't test too many things at once
- Don't change ads during first 2 weeks (learning phase)
- Don't remove performing headlines to test new ones

### When to Update
- **After 100+ clicks** to see performance trends
- **After learning phase** (usually 2 weeks)
- **When CTR < 3%** for search campaigns
- **When landing page changes** significantly

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. ❌ **Generic headlines:** "Best Solution for Your Business"
2. ❌ **No differentiation:** Could apply to any competitor
3. ❌ **Ignoring landing page:** Ad says X, page says Y
4. ❌ **Over-pinning:** Pinning 5+ headlines
5. ❌ **No variety:** All headlines are value props
6. ❌ **Wrong audience:** Developer copy for enterprise buyers
7. ❌ **Missing negatives:** Showing for "contact center jobs"
8. ❌ **Character limits:** Cutting off at "Build AI-Powered Con..."
