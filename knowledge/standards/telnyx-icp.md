# Telnyx ICP — Who We Sell To

*Last Updated: 2026-02-01*
*Source: ABM Scoring Logic*

---

## Primary Buyer Profile

**Senior technical and innovation leaders** responsible for deploying and scaling voice-based AI systems or communications infrastructure.

**Titles:**
- CTO, Head of Engineering, Chief Architect
- Chief Innovation Officer
- Founder / Co-founder (AI-native companies)
- VP Engineering, VP Platform, VP Product
- CEO, COO, VP Operations (secondary economic buyers)

**Mindset:**
- Infrastructure-literate and pragmatic
- Has already piloted or deployed Voice AI — understands models aren't the hard part
- Feels pain around telephony reliability, latency, global routing, and compliance
- Optimizing for long-term platform risk, not short-term demos

---

## Company Fit — Three Paths to ICP

### Path 1: Builds Voice/Telephony/Communications Products

Companies that **sell** communications as part of their product:
- Voicebots, virtual agents, AI calling agents
- Calling automation, IVR systems, agent assist
- STT/TTS products used in phone-based workflows
- Telephony APIs, programmable voice, CPaaS
- Communications infrastructure (SIP trunking, phone numbers, messaging, IoT connectivity)

**Examples:** Vertical AI agent vendors for healthcare, fintech, insurance, travel, hospitality, automotive, logistics

---

### Path 2: Runs Large-Scale Calling Operations

Companies that **operate** significant phone-based workflows internally:
- Contact centers and BPOs
- Outbound sales or collections teams
- Support, dispatch, or operations teams with high call volume

---

### Path 3: Relies on Phone/Connectivity as Core Operations

Companies where **phone or connectivity workflows are essential** to how they operate:
- Dispatch and logistics
- Field service coordination
- Time-sensitive rescheduling, confirmation, or verification via calls
- Telematics, device fleets, IoT connectivity

---

## Weak / Non-ICP Patterns

These companies are **deprioritized** even with high website engagement:

| Pattern | Why It's Weak |
|---------|---------------|
| Large IT/security vendors, analytics platforms | No clear voice/telephony products or call-heavy operations |
| Pharma, biotech, life sciences | Unless they have explicit contact centers or phone operations |
| AI transcription / media platforms | Focused on editing/content, not telephony or contact centers |
| Hardware/device manufacturers, hearing aids, consumer electronics | "Voice" is on-device UX, not phone calls |
| Telecoms, ISPs, carriers, MVNOs | Not aligned with Telnyx GTM motion |
| Traditional banks, credit unions | Unless they have explicit voice automation or contact center infrastructure |
| Generic SaaS (CRMs, booking tools, analytics) | Customers run the calls, not the vendor |
| IT integrators, consulting firms | Services, not product buyers |
| Utilities, government, public sector, education | Excluded verticals |

---

## Exclusions (Hard Suppression)

These accounts are **always deprioritized** regardless of engagement:

- Open Opportunities (already in pipeline)
- Stale Closed-Lost (no new engagement since close)
- Churned customers
- Direct competitors
- Self-serve tagged accounts
- Government, public sector, legal, education
- FCC Disqualified

---

## The Core Scoring Question

When evaluating any account, the scoring asks:

> **"Does this company build, sell, or operate voice/telephony/communications products or workflows — and are they showing real product evaluation behavior?"**

| Answer | Priority |
|--------|----------|
| Yes to both | **High priority** |
| Yes to one | Moderate priority |
| No to both | Low priority / nurture only |
