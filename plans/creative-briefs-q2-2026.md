### 30-Second Spot

**Concept:** "Voice AI Doesn't Fail Because of Models"

**Storyboard:**
- **0-5s:** Show the problem — garbled AI voice, latency gaps in conversation, a phone call dropping mid-sentence. Text overlay: "Voice AI doesn't fail because of models."
- **5-10s:** Show the real culprits — visualize carrier hops, public internet congestion, codec mismatches, SIP routing chaos. Text overlay: "It fails because telephony wasn't built for AI."
- **10-20s:** Transition — Telnyx infrastructure coming online. Animated private backbone connecting continents, edge GPUs spinning up at carrier PoPs, clean audio waveforms flowing through dedicated paths. Voice AI conversation plays back perfectly — natural, zero latency, crystal clear.
- **20-25s:** Show scale — numbers ticking up: "10,000 simultaneous AI calls. Global. Real-time."
- **25-30s:** End card — Telnyx logo + "We rebuilt the stack." + "The AI-Native Network" + telnyx.com

**Voiceover:** "Voice AI doesn't fail because of models. It fails because telephony wasn't built for AI. We rebuilt the stack from the network up. Telnyx. The AI-Native Network."

**Music/Sound:** Tension in first 5s (the problem), then resolves into confident, forward-moving score.

- Clean, technical aesthetic — think Apple infrastructure ads, not SaaS explainer videos
- Telnyx brand colors and visual language
- No stock footage of people in headsets or call centers
- Infrastructure is the hero — the network, the edge compute, the global backbone
- The AI voice quality difference should be *audible* — make the audience hear the contrast

## BRAND GUIDELINES REMINDER

**Messaging pillars (use across all creative):**
1. **Real-Time AI Performance** — edge GPUs, sub-200ms latency, natural conversation pacing
2. **Telephony Built for AI** — carrier-grade voice, HD audio, reliable SIP routing
3. **Voice That Drives Action** — AI that automates real workflows, not just answers questions
4. **Security & Trust** — SOC 2, HIPAA-ready, private network, no public internet exposure

**DO:**
- Lead with infrastructure confidence
- Use real technical language (developers are the audience)
- Show data and benchmarks
- Keep it clean, minimal, authoritative

**DON'T:**
- Use generic AI brain/robot imagery
- Use stock photos of people in headsets
- Use buzzwords without substance ("revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "game-changing")
- Make it look like every other SaaS ad
- Use competitor logos (reference them in copy only, not visuals)
